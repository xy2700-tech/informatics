NYC Public Arts & Culture Atlas

Mapping access to non-profit arts and cultural organizations across New York City boroughs.

Xinyu (Kelly) Yan | Urban Data and Informatics, Fall 2025 | Columbia GSAPP

About

This projects uses NYC Department of Cultural Affairs data of non-profit arts and culture organizations in New York City. It maps over 1,500 non-profit arts and cultural organizations throughout New York City's five boroughs. From community theaters to major museums, each organization plays a vital role in the city's cultural ecosystem. By visualizing their locations and concentrations, this atlas reveals patterns in cultural density and highlights disparities in arts access across different neighborhoods.

Analysis

Drag around the map to see areas with high counts of arts organizations. Each extrusion is color coded to show predominant discipline type.

  • Manhattan Concentration: 45% of all cultural organizations are located in Manhattan
  • Gallery Districts: Chelsea and Williamsburg show the highest density of visual arts organizations
  • Theater Hub: Over 200 performance venues concentrate in Midtown and Lower Manhattan
  • Access Gaps: Manhattan averages 12 organizations per neighborhood vs. 3-5 in outer boroughs
  • Cultural Deserts: Several neighborhoods in outer Queens and Staten Island have zero organizations

Manhattan alone brings up the average count.

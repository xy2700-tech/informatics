NYC Non-profit Arts & Culture Atlas

Mapping access to non-profit arts and cultural organizations across New York City boroughs.

About

New York City is known internationally for its artistic and cultural vibrancy, as residents and tourists alike frequent venues from city-owned institutions to private galleries. This project specifically focuses on the neighborhood level arts and culture ecosystem, highlighting the spatial distribution of non-profit arts and culture organizations. Data is pulled from the NYC Department of Cultural Affairs directory of cultural organizations, organized by discipline, and geocoded for visual clarity. A webscraper appended website links to the organization dataframe, which is then merged with neighborhood tabulation areas.

Analysis

Drag around the map to compare arts and culture density, hover over the extrusions to view neighborhood name and count.

  • Manhattan Concentration: 45% of all cultural organizations are located in Manhattan
  • Gallery Districts: Chelsea and Williamsburg show the highest density of visual arts organizations
  • Theater Hub: Over 200 performance venues concentrate in Midtown and Lower Manhattan

A huge access gap exists as Manhattan averages 12 organizations per neighborhood versus 3-5 in outer boroughs.
